Climate Anomalies Visualizations

By Akshar Katariya

One of the most important datasets on climate change has come from the NCEI NOAA. And it is very simple — just the month, the year, and the average temperature anomaly for that month and year, all the way from the 1850s to the present.

Year Month Anomaly (°C)
Some people have made incredible transformations of this simple data into truly powerful visualizations and animations (check climate spiral and the warming stripes).

I got introduced to this dataset during a climate science and policy class. Students were asked to think deeply about creating a compelling and world-changing visual output from it. This was a really tough task, especially since the spiral and stripes pretty much do it all.

I wanted to push myself creatively and decided to make at least five distinctive and creative plots from that same data.

This plot shows monthly climate anomalies across different decades, highlighting seasonal trends and temperature variability over time.

This 3D surface plot visualizes a complex dataset (e.g., volcano topography or climate variation) to uncover patterns not easily seen in 2D plots.

This audio summary provides a verbal walkthrough of key patterns observed in the year-month anomaly dataset.

This chart likely represents climate anomalies through a typographic lens, encoding temperature deviation data using letterforms for a creative visual twist.

This thermometer-style plot gives an intuitive view of how recorded anomalies align with temperature ranges—like reading a historical climate thermometer.
